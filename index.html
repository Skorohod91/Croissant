Crescent-shaped flour products (German: Kipferl) have been baked in Austria since at least the 13th century. But the beginning of the current popularity of croissants can be considered 1839, when the Austrian artillery officer August Tsang (German: August Zang) opened the Viennese Bakery (French: Boulangerie Viennoise) in Paris, where such buns were baked.
